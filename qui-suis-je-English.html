Professional Background

My name is Alexis Chabanne, a graduate in Computer Science (BUT) – Application Development: Design, Development, Validation track – from IUT2 Grenoble. I completed my third year abroad as part of a university exchange at UQAC (Université du Québec à Chicoutimi), within a double-degree program. This experience allowed me to grow in a multicultural, stimulating, and demanding environment while strengthening both my technical and interpersonal skills.

My academic path allowed me to explore a wide range of fields, from web and mobile development to artificial intelligence, including project management, database modeling, and deployment environments. Among the most significant courses I took were:

  • Machine Learning for Data Science
  • Advanced Web Technologies
  • Mobile Computing
  • Algorithms and Data Structures
  • Advanced Databases (PostgreSQL, MongoDB, etc.)
  • Application Deployment Environments
  • Production Management and Agile Teamwork

I applied these skills in both academic and personal projects, notably by developing a sports event management application (“Step by Step”) and a community website focused on sports. The latter combines my two main passions: computing and sports.

As a lifelong athlete, I have played football in clubs since childhood and have been practicing running (marathon, trail) for the past two years. During my year in Québec, I joined the university athletics program, which allowed me to continue progressing while developing discipline, perseverance, and team spirit — fundamental qualities both on the field and in the professional world.

Admitted for the 2025 academic year to ENSIBS (École Nationale Supérieure d’Ingénieurs de Bretagne Sud), and currently in the process of admission to Polytech, I aim to deepen my knowledge in software systems engineering, with a focus on impactful, robust, and meaningful projects.

I am open to opportunities in software development, data science, application architecture, or the design of web and mobile solutions — whether through internships, work-study programs, or collaborations. Serious, curious, adaptable, and committed, I am ready to take on new technological challenges.

My International Year

In 2024–2025, I completed a university exchange year abroad as part of a double-degree program at the Université du Québec à Chicoutimi (UQAC). This international mobility allowed me to validate the third year of my BUT in Computer Science while working toward a Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science under the North American system.

Alongside my studies, I joined the Inuk university athletics team, where I took part in competitive running with intensive supervised training. This experience allowed me to cultivate values of endurance, performance, and resilience. I even won my first race there.

I also held a position as a footwear advisor at Sport Expert Chicoutimi. This student job exposed me to the demands of customer service, inventory management, and technical advice, in a fast-paced and demanding environment.

Balancing full-time studies, a student job, and a high-level athletic commitment required rigorous organization, optimal time management, and strong resilience. The ability to maintain a balance between academic, athletic, and professional performance stands as one of the key accomplishments of my year.

Finally, this mobility was an opportunity to discover the vast landscapes of Québec: I explored the surrounding nature through hiking, ski touring, and cross-country skiing. These activities allowed me to fully experience the Canadian winter and to strengthen my connection with the environment — a fundamental aspect of my personal balance.

“Studying, working, and training in Québec was a full immersion: I discovered rigor, resilience, and the rich human and cultural fabric of Canada.”

What this year taught me:

  • Autonomy: managing my studies, job, and training with discipline
  • Resilience: maintaining a high level of commitment despite personal and weather-related challenges
  • Multiculturality: living and learning in a North American context with different practices
  • Performance: achieving my academic and athletic goals, including a competition victory
  • Adaptability: quickly integrating into a new university, a new team, and a new job
